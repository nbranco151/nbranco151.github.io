About Nicholas Branco

Data Scientist

While data science is my main area of study now, it was not always that way. I began my academic career as an undergraduate in Stockton University (then known as the Richard Stockton College of New Jersey) as a physics major with a minor in mathematics. I had loved physics in high school, so it was only natural I continue to study in that field. During my studies, I learned how to use MATLAB. Initially thinking the program was a glorified calculator, it didn't dawn on me until I began my undergraduate research that it was an extremely useful tool for modeling and studying systems. Later on, I was introduced to Python and I immediately recognized how useful it was. I loved using it so much, I decided to apply to Stockton's Data Science and Strategical Analytics master's program at the recommendation of one of my professors, Dr. John Russel Manson, since the program made use of python extensively. I graduated this program in August 2018, achieving my Masters Degree in Data Science and Strategical Analytics.

Now, I am the field of data science, and I haven't looked back since. As much as I love physics, this line of work fits me better. The skills I learned as an undergraduate are still applied every day, so it wasn't a total waste! My main interests are in network science, which was a part of my undergraduate and graduate projects.

Currently, I am working as a Systems Coordinator for the Food and Beverage department of Hard Rock Hotel and Casino in Atlantic City.
